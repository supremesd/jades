Kepler-186f

Kepler-186f is an exoplanet located approximately 500 light-years away in the constellation of Cygnus. It is notable for being the first Earth-sized planet discovered in the habitable zone of a star similar to our Sun. Orbiting the K-type dwarf star Kepler-186, it has a radius about 1.1 times that of Earth and completes its orbit in roughly 130 days. While its atmospheric composition is unknown, its position within the habitable zone suggests it could support liquid water, making it a prime candidate for habitability. The discovery of Kepler-186f by the Kepler Space Telescope in 2014 is significant as it enhances our understanding of exoplanetary systems and the potential for life beyond Earth.
